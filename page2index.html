Mizoram

Mizoram is a state in the northeastern part of India, known for its rolling hills, valleys, rivers, and pleasant climate. It shares borders with Myanmar, Bangladesh, and the Indian states of Manipur, Assam, and Tripura. The capital is Aizawl, and Mizo is the official language. Mizoram is often called the “Land of the Hill People” and is home to a rich tribal culture with vibrant festivals, traditional dances, and handicrafts. Agriculture, especially jhum (shifting) cultivation, plays a key role in its economy. The state is also known for its bamboo forests, wildlife sanctuaries, and scenic spots like Phawngpui (Blue Mountain), making it a peaceful and picturesque destination.
